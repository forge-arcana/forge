## THE ELEVATOR PITCH

> There are 1.1 million sari-sari stores in the Philippines — and not a single one is searchable online. If you need a sachet of shampoo at 9 PM, your only option is to walk from store to store and hope someone has it. Sookie turns every sari-sari store into a digital storefront: store owners snap a photo of their shelves, AI builds their inventory, and customers search for any product and instantly see which nearby store has it in stock. Same walk-to-store experience. No more wasted trips.

## THE PROBLEM

### Maria's Story

Maria lives in a residential subdivision in Quezon City. It's 8:30 PM. She's about to shower and realizes she's out of Cream Silk conditioner. She slips on her sandals and walks to the nearest sari-sari store — the one at the corner, 40 meters away. Closed. She tries the next one, two blocks down. Open, but no Cream Silk. The owner suggests Head & Shoulders. That's not what she wants.

She keeps walking. Third store — also no Cream Silk. Fourth store — closed. Fifth store has it, but only in the wrong variant. She's now 15 minutes into a walk she expected to take 2 minutes. She tries three more stores. Nothing. After 30 minutes and 10 stores visited, she gives up. She showers without conditioner and adds it to her mental list for tomorrow's grocery run at SM — where she'll spend ₱400 on impulse purchases she didn't plan for.

Meanwhile, Aling Nena — a store owner six houses from Maria's — has three sachets of Cream Silk sitting on her shelf. She'll close at 10 PM tonight with those sachets unsold. She has no way to tell Maria they're there. No sign, no listing, no digital presence. Just a physical store that you can only discover by walking past it.

### The Problem at Scale

Maria and Aling Nena aren't unique. The Philippines has **over 1.1 million sari-sari stores** — the densest retail network in Southeast Asia. They serve **over 90% of Filipino households** and account for roughly **40-50% of all fast-moving consumer goods (FMCG) sales** in the country. That's an estimated **₱1.5-2 trillion per year** flowing through stores that have zero digital presence.

No inventory visibility. No searchability. No way for a customer to know what's in stock without physically walking there. In 2026, the most important retail channel in the Philippines is completely invisible online.

## THE SOLUTION

### How Sookie Works

- **Store owners snap a photo** → AI recognizes products on the shelves and generates a digital inventory automatically. No typing. No barcodes. No data entry.
- **Customers search for any product** → Type "Cream Silk" and instantly see which nearby stores have it, how far away they are, and the price. No more walking store to store.
- **Reserve and walk in** → Found what you need? Reserve it so it's held for you. Walk to the store, pick it up, and pay through the app.
- **Inventory stays current** → Sales through Sookie automatically update stock. When the owner restocks, they snap another photo and the app asks them to confirm. Simple manual adjustments are always available.
- **Owners see their store like never before** → For the first time, a sari-sari store owner has a digital view of their inventory, their sales, and what's running low — without spreadsheets, notebooks, or guesswork.

### Maria's Day — After Sookie

It's 8:30 PM. Maria needs Cream Silk. She opens Sookie, types "Cream Silk," and sees three stores within 200 meters that have it. Aling Nena's store — 40 meters away — has it for ₱8. Maria taps "Reserve," walks over in 90 seconds, picks it up, pays through the app, and is back home in under 5 minutes. Done.

Aling Nena? She gets a notification: "Maria reserved Cream Silk (₱8). Pickup in ~2 minutes." The sachet that would've sat unsold is now revenue. And three more customers she's never seen before found her store this week through Sookie — all searching for items she happened to have in stock. Her daily sales are up ₱200, and she didn't change a thing about how she runs her store.

### Why Now?

- **AI image recognition is production-ready.** For the first time, a store owner can photograph a shelf and get an accurate product inventory without manual data entry. This was science fiction two years ago. It's a phone camera feature today.
- **Smartphone saturation among store owners.** 96% of Filipino adults own a smartphone — including sari-sari store owners. The hardware required to run Sookie already exists in every store owner's pocket.
- **AI-driven development collapses build costs.** The technical barrier that killed similar attempts before — building and maintaining a reliable platform at this scale — is no longer a barrier. AI-assisted development means a small expert team can build and maintain what previously required a funded engineering department.
- **FMCG data hunger.** Unilever, P&G, and Nestlé have spent years trying to get real-time visibility into the sari-sari channel. They've run pilots, hired field agents, and partnered with distributors — but nobody has cracked real-time, ground-truth inventory data at scale. Sookie generates this data as a byproduct.

### Go-to-Market

**Phase 1 — One Barangay (2026):** Launch in a single dense residential barangay in Metro Manila — ideally one where the founding team has personal relationships with 10-20 store owners. Onboard 30-50 stores within a 1km radius. At this density, every household search returns results. Prove the model: stores see more sales, customers find what they need.

**Phase 2 — Metro Manila Barangays (2027):** Replicate the barangay playbook across 20-30 neighborhoods. Target areas with high sari-sari density and strong smartphone adoption. Reach 2,000+ active stores.

**Phase 3 — National Urban (2028+):** Expand to Cebu, Davao, and other urban centers. Open a self-service onboarding flow where any store owner can photograph their store and go live without field support.

## THE BUSINESS MODEL

### How We Make Money

1. **Convenience fee (Fibonacci session tiers)** — Customers choose a session pass before shopping. The longer the window, the better the value:

   | Tier | Fee | Window | Cost/Hour |
   |------|-----|--------|-----------|
   | Quick | ₱1 | 1 hour | ₱1.00 |
   | Evening | ₱2 | 3 hours | ₱0.67 |
   | Half-Day | ₱3 | 5 hours | ₱0.60 |
   | Full Day | ₱5 | 8 hours | ₱0.63 |
   | All Day | ₱8 | 13 hours | ₱0.62 |
   | Unlimited | ₱13 | 21 hours | ₱0.62 |

   Within the window, all searches, reservations, and purchases are unlimited. The Fibonacci scaling creates a natural "why not pay a little more?" upsell — customers who came for one sachet realize they save by bundling their shopping into a longer session. The ₱1 entry point is frictionless; the ₱5-8 tiers capture power shoppers.
2. **Premium subscription (Store owners)** — Monthly subscription (target: ₱199-499/month) for advanced inventory management: stock analytics, low-stock alerts, demand forecasting, restocking recommendations, and sales reporting. Free tier covers basic inventory and transactions.
3. **Data and advertising (Future)** — Anonymized, aggregated inventory and sales data across thousands of stores. FMCG brands pay for real-time channel visibility: what's selling where, what's out of stock, which products move fastest by geography. Promoted product placements in search results.

| Delivery model | Walk yourself | Rider delivery (markup) | B2B delivery | **Walk yourself (no markup)** |
| Store owner cost | None | Commission (25-30%) | None | **Free tier available** |
| Real-time stock accuracy | Ask the owner | Managed catalog | Distributor-reported | **AI-verified, sale-decremented** |

### Our Moat

- **Network effects:** Every store on Sookie makes the platform more useful to nearby customers (more search results). Every customer makes it more valuable for stores (more sales). Critical mass in a barangay creates a local lock-in that's extremely hard for a newcomer to break.
- **Data asset:** Every transaction and inventory scan builds the most granular real-time map of FMCG retail distribution in the Philippines. This data doesn't exist anywhere else — not in Nielsen panels, not in distributor records, not in FMCG headquarters. It compounds daily and becomes exponentially more valuable.
- **AI-first execution:** The founding team's deep AI expertise means they can build and iterate on the core inventory recognition system faster and cheaper than a competitor starting from scratch. Copying the idea is easy. Maintaining accurate AI-driven inventory at scale, reliably, is the real moat.

## RISKS & MITIGATION

| Risk | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|------------|
| **Store owner adoption resistance** — "I don't need an app, my customers know where I am" | High | Free tier with zero effort onboarding (one photo). Show concrete value: "3 new customers found you this week." Start with tech-friendly owners in founder's network. |
| **AI inventory accuracy is unreliable** — misidentifies products, wrong counts | Medium | Human-in-the-loop: AI generates, owner verifies. Accuracy improves with every correction. Start with common FMCG products (high recognition confidence) before expanding to niche items. |
| **Customers won't pay session fee** — "I'll just walk for free" | Medium | Free period builds habit first. ₱1 entry point is virtually frictionless — less than a piece of candy. Position as insurance: "Know before you go." Fibonacci tiers let customers self-select value. A/B test tier pricing and windows before committing. |
| **Can't achieve barangay density** — too few stores per area, search returns no results | High | Hyper-local launch: one barangay at a time, 30+ stores minimum before marketing to customers. Never launch in an area without sufficient store coverage. |
| **Complaints and bad publicity** — bad experience goes viral, damages brand trust | Medium | Obsessive early-user support. Founder-led customer service in pilot phase. Fix problems in hours, not days. Every complaint is a product improvement. Build trust store-by-store, not city-wide. |
