## Numeric Sections as Signed Ledger Entries; Soft-Landing Stop-the-Bet (2026-07-04)

**Learning**: When a product's Touchstone carries an accounting/ledger motif, rendering the Pitch's three numeric sections (Field, Stake, Signal) as ruled three-column tables (claim / honest version / ballpark) with a double-rule closing row makes founder-honesty visible as form: each claim reads like an entry the founder must countersign, and the stop-the-bet clause landing in the ledger's closing row carries the weight of a balanced total. Separately: a soft-landing stop-the-bet ("the engine remains the house's own tool") is more honest AND more credible than a dramatic kill clause when the product is dogfooded infrastructure — the founder genuinely loses little by stopping, which makes the stop signal believable rather than performative.

**Apply when**: Rendering any Pitch whose aesthetic supports tabular/ledger form; and whenever the product is dogfooded infrastructure, prefer a soft-landing stop-the-bet over theatrical kill language.
