Our Philosophy

Coffee is
a conversation.

"Every cup carries the hands that grew it, the heat that shaped it, and the silence between you and the first sip."

We source directly from independent farms across Ethiopia, Colombia, and Costa Rica — building long-term relationships with growers who share our obsession with terroir. Every batch is roasted in small quantities on our vintage 12-kg drum, dialed in by feel as much as by data.

Ember & Oak is not a chain. It's not a concept. It's a room with good light, honest coffee, and enough quiet to hear yourself think.

The Craft

From source
to cup.

We don't buy commodity coffee. We build long-term relationships with producers who farm with intention — and we pay the price that makes that intention sustainable.

01

Direct Sourcing

We visit every farm we work with at least once. We cup on-site, meet the pickers, and understand what we're asking of the land before we ask it.

02

Small-Batch Roasting

12-kilogram batches, roasted twice weekly. We use a first-crack-focused profile — no second crack, no dark roast. The fruit stays alive. The terroir stays intact.

03

Seven-Day Rest

All roasted coffee rests seven days before reaching the bar. CO₂ off-gassing is real. Patience improves every brew variable. We wait because it matters.

04

Dial-In Every Day

Baristas pull test shots every morning. Grind size, dose, yield — calibrated to the day's humidity, the bean's age, and what our palates say. Never the same settings twice.

"Specialty coffee is the only food category where the farmer's name should be on the cup. We're just the middle chapter."
