## THE ELEVATOR PITCH

> Food delivery apps mark up restaurant prices by 20-30% — so customers skip ordering and restaurants lose sales. Kain! is a zero-markup food delivery app that outsources all deliveries to existing platforms like Grab and Lalamove. With near-zero operations, we pass the savings to customers and list every item at its actual restaurant price. Same food, same price, delivered.

---

## THE PROBLEM

### Mark's Story

Mark is an office worker in Makati. It's 12:30 PM, he's hungry, and he's craving chicken inasal from his favorite carinderia three blocks away. But it's raining, the line will be long, and he's got a meeting at 1 PM.

He opens GrabFood. The inasal meal he usually buys for ₱120 walk-in is listed at ₱155. Add the delivery fee, and he's looking at nearly ₱200 for a ₱120 meal. He closes the app. He makes instant coffee from the office pantry and waits until he can go out later.

Meanwhile, Aling Rosa — the carinderia owner — is staring at her GrabFood dashboard wondering why she only gets 5 online orders a day when her walk-in line stretches out the door every lunch. She finds out why: to cover GrabFood's 25-30% commission, her prices on the app are marked up. Her loyal customers see the higher prices and just don't order. She's paying for a platform that prices out her own regulars.

### The Problem at Scale

Mark and Aling Rosa aren't alone. There are over **3 million daily food delivery users** in Metro Manila, and studies show that **60-70% of potential orders are abandoned** due to price sensitivity. Restaurants lose online revenue. Customers go hungry or settle. Delivery platforms collect commissions on shrinking order volumes. Everyone loses.

---

## THE SOLUTION

### How Kain! Works

- **Browse restaurants** → See the exact same prices as the physical menu. ₱120 in the store? ₱120 on Kain!.
- **Place your order** → Kain! sends the order to the restaurant and automatically dispatches a rider through an existing delivery platform's API.
- **Pay a transparent delivery fee** → No hidden markups on food. You only pay for delivery, clearly separated.
- **Get your food** → Delivered by a rider from Grab, Lalamove, or whichever platform has the best availability and price in your area.

### Mark's Day — After Kain!

It's 12:30 PM again. Mark opens Kain!. The chicken inasal is ₱120 — the same price he pays when he walks in. Delivery fee: ₱55. Total: ₱175. He doesn't think twice. He orders. Twenty minutes later, he's eating his favorite meal at his desk, dry and full, with five minutes to spare before his meeting.

Aling Rosa? Her online orders tripled. Same prices, same margins, more customers. She didn't have to change anything.

| **TAM** (Total Addressable Market) | ~₱180B/year (~$3.2B) | Philippine online food delivery market, estimated 15M+ potential users nationwide at avg. ₱250/order |
| **SAM** (Serviceable Addressable Market) | ~₱80B/year (~$1.4B) | Metro Manila food delivery market, ~3M active delivery users |
| **SOM** (Year 1-2 Target) | ~₱500M/year (~$9M) | Capture 0.6% of Metro Manila delivery orders through price advantage |

### Why Now?

- **Delivery API availability:** For the first time, platforms like Grab and Lalamove offer public or accessible APIs that allow third parties to programmatically book deliveries. This was nearly impossible just months ago.
- **Price sensitivity at an all-time high:** Inflation and cost of living increases have made Filipino consumers hyper-aware of markups. The appetite for affordable delivery has never been stronger.
- **Food delivery is mainstream:** Post-pandemic, food delivery is no longer a novelty — it's a daily habit. The behavior exists. The barrier is price.

### Go-to-Market

Launch in Metro Manila — the densest food delivery market in the Philippines. Expand to Cebu, Davao, and other metro areas based on delivery API availability and order volume data from initial rollout.

## THE BUSINESS MODEL

### How We Make Money

1. **Delivery fee margin** — Kain! charges customers a delivery fee that includes a spread above the actual cost paid to the outsourced delivery platform. This is the primary revenue stream.
2. **Premium subscription** — A monthly subscription tier offering discounted or free delivery for frequent users. Drives order volume and predictable recurring revenue.
3. **Future: Restaurant promotion tools** — Paid visibility, featured listings, and promotional placements for restaurants wanting to boost their online orders.

## THE COMPETITION

### Landscape

| | GrabFood | Foodpanda | **Kain!** |
|--|----------|-----------|-----------|
| Food pricing | Marked up 20-30% | Marked up 20-30% | **Restaurant price — zero markup** |
| Revenue model | Restaurant commission (25-30%) | Restaurant commission (25-30%) | **Delivery fee margin only** |
| Delivery fleet | Own riders (massive opex) | Own riders (massive opex) | **Outsourced via API (near-zero opex)** |
| Customer incentive to order | Convenience only | Convenience only | **Convenience + affordability** |
| Restaurant margin impact | Erodes margins or forces markup | Erodes margins or forces markup | **No impact — same margins as walk-in** |

### Our Moat

- **Network effects:** More restaurants attract more customers. More customers attract more restaurants. Once Kain! reaches critical mass in Metro Manila, a competitor starts from zero with neither side of the marketplace.
- **Data advantage:** Every order teaches us which delivery APIs perform best in which areas, at what times, at what cost. This operational intelligence compounds — we get faster, cheaper, and more reliable over time.
- **Symbiotic positioning:** We're not competing with Grab — we're their customer. They earn delivery fees from every Kain! order. They have no incentive to shut us down, and every incentive to support us.
