## THE ELEVATOR PITCH

> You know how millions of Filipino commuters still fumble for exact change every time they ride a jeepney — and drivers lose fares to miscounting every single day? Jeepi turns every jeepney into a cashless, GPS-tracked ride: passengers board with a QR scan, pay automatically when they get off, and drivers see every peso earned in real time. We're bringing the reliability of modern transit payment to the Philippines' most iconic — and most underserved — mode of public transport.

## THE PROBLEM

### Maria's Story

Maria is a 28-year-old call center agent in Quezon City. Every morning at 5:45 AM, she walks to the jeepney stop on Commonwealth Avenue and waits. When the jeepney arrives, she climbs in, digs through her bag for exact change — ₱13 for the first 4 kilometers. She doesn't have coins. She hands the driver a ₱100 bill. The driver, both hands on the wheel in traffic, takes the bill, mentally calculates change, and passes ₱87 back through a chain of passengers. Sometimes the change is wrong. Maria doesn't argue — she's already running late.

On the way home, she takes a longer route. She's not sure when she's 4 km past the base fare. The driver charges ₱28. Was that right? She doesn't know. She pays. If the driver shortchanges her ₱5, that's ₱5 × twice a day × 22 workdays = ₱2,200 a year she'll never get back. That's more than a day's wage.

Meanwhile, Mang Boy — her driver — has his own problems. He handles 300+ cash transactions a day while navigating Manila traffic. At the end of his shift, he counts crumpled bills and guesses his daily take. He knows some fares slipped through. He knows some passengers underpaid. But he can't prove it, and he can't track it. His operator asks for the daily "boundary" (fixed daily rental fee) and doesn't care about the math. Mang Boy earns less than he should, every single day.

### The Problem at Scale

Maria and Mang Boy aren't unique. The Philippines has **over 200,000 jeepneys** carrying an estimated **40 million passenger trips daily** — making it the country's largest public transit system. Every single transaction is cash. There is no receipt, no record, no tracking. Philippine law mandates 20% fare discounts for students, seniors, and persons with disabilities — but enforcement is inconsistent because there's no system to verify eligibility or ensure the discount is applied.

The jeepney fare economy moves an estimated **₱22 billion per year** ($400M+) in Metro Manila alone — entirely in cash, entirely untracked, entirely unaccountable.

## THE SOLUTION

### How Jeepi Works

- **Board with a tap** — Passengers scan a QR code on the jeepney. No app download required the first time, no fumbling for coins. The system knows which jeepney you're on.
- **Ride without worrying** — GPS tracks the jeepney's position in real time. Passengers see exactly where they are on the route and how much the fare will be. No guessing, no overpaying.
- **Get off and go** — Press "Para Po!" (the Filipino term for requesting a stop). The system calculates the exact fare based on GPS distance, deducts it from the digital wallet, and refunds any excess hold. Done.
- **Drivers earn transparently** — Every fare is recorded instantly. Drivers see a live earnings dashboard. At the end of the shift, there's no counting coins — just a total and an instant cashout option.
- **Discounts applied automatically** — Students, seniors, and PWDs upload their IDs once. The 20% discount is applied on every ride, every time — no awkward conversations with drivers.

### Maria's Day — After Jeepi

Maria opens her phone, scans the QR code on the jeepney's windshield, and sits down. Her phone shows "Boarded — Commonwealth Ave, Stop 7." She puts in her earbuds. When she's two stops from her office, her phone buzzes: "Approaching Stop 12." She presses "Para Po!" The screen shows: "Fare: ₱24.40. Wallet balance: ₱312.60." She steps off. No cash touched. No change miscounted. She didn't even take her wallet out of her bag.

Mang Boy glances at his dashboard between stops. "14 passengers today. ₱387 earned. 3 discount fares applied." At the end of his shift, he taps "Cash Out" and ₱2,847 lands in his GCash account in seconds. For the first time in 18 years of driving, he knows exactly what he earned today — to the centavo.

### Why Now?

- **Government push for modernization:** The Philippine government's PUV Modernization Program is pushing jeepneys toward consolidation under fleet operators — creating a natural B2B sales channel. Operators want fleet analytics that only digital fare systems can provide.
- **Mobile wallet adoption explosion:** GCash and Maya now have **76M+ registered users** in a country of 110M. Filipinos are already comfortable with digital payments — just not on jeepneys yet.
- **Regulatory tailwind:** BSP (Central Bank) is actively promoting cashless payments. Recent circulars encourage e-money adoption in public transit. The Beep card system exists for trains but hasn't reached jeepneys — the gap is wide open.
- **Smartphone penetration:** 96% of Filipino adults own a smartphone. The hardware barrier is gone — both drivers and passengers already have the device they need.

## THE BUSINESS MODEL

### How We Make Money

1. **Passenger convenience fee** — ₱1.00 per boarding. Paid by the passenger on fare settlement. (Discounted to ₱0.50 for students/seniors/PWDs per law.)
2. **Driver settlement fee** — ₱0.20 per fare settlement. Deducted from driver earnings.
3. **Wallet reload margin** — 1-2% spread on digital wallet top-ups via payment gateway (GCash, Maya, cards). Passed through from Xendit gateway fees.
4. **Future: Operator subscription** — Monthly SaaS fee for fleet analytics dashboard, compliance reporting, and route optimization tools. (Phase 2+)
5. **Future: Data licensing** — Anonymized transit data for urban planners, LGUs, and advertisers. (Phase 3+)

| Discount enforcement | Honor system | Card-based tier | N/A | **KYC-verified, auto-applied** |
| Driver earnings tracking | None | None | N/A | **Real-time dashboard + cashout** |
| Infrastructure required | None | Terminal hardware per vehicle (~₱15K) | N/A | **Phone only (already owned)** |
| Fleet analytics | None | Operator-level only | Route data | **Per-jeepney, per-driver, per-route** |

### Our Moat

- **Network effects:** Every jeepney with Jeepi makes the platform more useful to passengers (more routes available), and every passenger makes it more attractive to drivers (more cashless fares). The flywheel strengthens with each new route.
- **Zero-hardware model:** Competitors like Beep require ₱15,000+ in terminal hardware per jeepney. Jeepi runs on phones that drivers and passengers already own. This makes fleet onboarding 100x cheaper and faster.
- **Regulatory compliance built in:** RA 9994/10754/11314 discount enforcement, BSP e-money tier compliance, and AMLA monitoring are baked into the platform. Competitors would need to rebuild this from scratch for the Philippine market.
- **Data advantage:** Every trip generates GPS telemetry, fare data, and route analytics that no one else has for jeepneys. This data becomes a strategic asset for urban planning partnerships and route optimization.

## RISKS & MITIGATION

| Risk | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|------------|
| Driver adoption resistance ("I prefer cash") | High | Zero cost to drivers. Instant cashout to GCash/Maya. Frame as "you earn MORE because no fares slip through." Start with progressive operators who mandate it for their fleet. |
| Regulatory delay (BSP EMI license) | Medium | Platform can operate as a technology provider to a licensed e-money issuer. Partnership with existing EMI (GCash/Maya) as interim path. |
| Connectivity gaps (no internet in some areas) | Medium | Offline queue built in — transactions sync when connection returns. QR boarding works offline. |
| Competitor entry (Grab/GCash builds jeepney payments) | Low | They've had years and haven't. Jeepneys are a hyper-local, fragmented market that big platforms deprioritize. Our ground-up approach (GPS fare calc, driver dashboard, discount compliance) is hard to replicate as a feature add-on. |
| Passenger trust ("Where's my money?") | Medium | Real-time wallet balance visible at all times. Hold-settle model means passengers see the max charge upfront and get a refund of the difference. Full transaction history. |
